### Reflections on Unresolved Considerations

Looking at the arguments presented so far, one can't help but wonder whether the timing question is really as clear-cut as it appears. On one hand, the protocol-first window analysis suggests urgency, but on the other hand, it's worth asking: do we really know how fast model providers will consolidate their context stores? The evidence points in multiple directions, and more observation may be needed before drawing firm conclusions.

The sparse RL signal problem raised in compression is certainly worth thinking about. Individual purchase signals are infrequent, as noted, and collaborative filtering introduces re-centralization risks, as also noted. Whether this tension can be resolved depends on a number of factors that remain, at this stage, somewhat indeterminate. It's possible that hybrid approaches could work, but it's equally possible they couldn't — the question merits further exploration.

Regarding the business model, Scenario D (brand pays per context-informed conversion) was identified as the most compelling revenue model, and there's something to that reasoning. Then again, one might ask whether conversion attribution in an agentic context is straightforward or whether it introduces its own measurement challenges. Attribution has historically been contested even in traditional digital advertising, so it stands to reason that agentic attribution could be similarly complex — though perhaps not.

The MCP precedent is interesting and does seem to lower technical barriers, as the expansion pass argued. At the same time, the existence of a technical foundation doesn't necessarily guarantee adoption, as we've seen with other open protocols that had solid technical underpinnings but failed to achieve critical mass. Whether MCP-based user context servers would follow the successful pattern (OAuth) or the unsuccessful pattern (Solid Project) is genuinely hard to say at this point.

Finally, the question of agent quality as a bottleneck deserves continued attention. The thesis's value proposition is gated by agent shopping capability, and current agents are, as discussed, not yet reliable enough. How quickly this improves will significantly shape the opportunity timeline, though predictions about AI capability timelines have historically been difficult to make with precision.

## CRITIQUE PASS

### Strongest Unresolved Questions

1. **Does the thesis conflate two distinct markets?** There's a meaningful difference between *high-consideration purchases* (running shoes, electronics) and *commodity replenishment* (paper towels, toothpaste). Agent-mediated commerce will likely dominate replenishment first, where preferences are simple and stable. But replenishment is precisely the category where rich RL context matters least — you already know what you want. The high-consideration purchases where context matters most are the ones humans will resist delegating to agents longest. This creates a **value-timing paradox** the thesis hasn't addressed.

2. **What about the attention economy's migration to entertainment?** The thesis focuses on commerce disruption but ignores that advertising spend may simply redirect toward the remaining human-attention surfaces — streaming, gaming, social video. If ad dollars flow from search/shopping to TikTok and YouTube rather than disappearing, the "impression collapse" is really an "impression migration." The total disruption to the digital economy may be far less dramatic than the thesis implies.

3. **Is collaborative filtering the real moat, not portability?** The sparse RL problem identified in expansion suggests that individual context is insufficient. The real value may be in *networks of similar users* — which is exactly what Amazon already has at scale. A portable context protocol with 100K users loses to Amazon's collaborative signal from 300M purchasers. The thesis may be arguing for the wrong layer entirely — the scarce resource isn't individual preferences but population-level taste graphs.

4. **Could the "Stripe for context" become a liability magnet?** Centralized stores of consumer preference data create a single point of regulatory and legal exposure. CCPA, GDPR, and emerging AI-specific regulations could make operating this layer extraordinarily expensive from a compliance standpoint — a cost structure very different from Stripe's payment processing model.

5. **Where does trust calibration fit?** The thesis assumes users want agents to act autonomously. But trust in AI decision-making follows a domain-specific curve — people trust GPS navigation but not AI medical diagnosis. Shopping falls somewhere in between. The rate of trust adoption may determine the entire thesis's relevance more than any infrastructure question.

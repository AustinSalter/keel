### Elevation Search

It's worth reflecting on where we've arrived after three iterations of exploration and compression. The gradient model of frame selection — ranging from automatable low-level selection to durably valuable high-level meta-judgment — is certainly an interesting development. But one might ask: is this gradient itself the right way to think about the problem, or is it perhaps just one lens among many?

On the one hand, the distinction between low-level and high-level frame selection feels intuitively compelling. Claude Code automating project-level context is clearly different from a human deciding to read an investment problem through Hegelian dialectics. On the other hand, where exactly do you draw the line? Couldn't it be argued that what seems like "high-level" judgment today might look like straightforward pattern matching tomorrow? More investigation would be needed to say with certainty.

The information-theoretic formulation raised in compression — measuring mutual information between context and output quality — is potentially promising. But is it truly the right metric? Would mutual information capture the kind of frame-breaking insights that the 25% meta-level elevation finding describes, or would it only measure incremental improvements within a fixed frame? These are questions worth considering, though definitive answers remain elusive at this stage.

Similarly, the observation that the thesis produced roughly 40-50% genuine alpha from cross-domain philosophical transfer raises its own questions. Is 40-50% a good hit rate? It depends on the baseline, of course. And what counts as "genuine alpha" versus "suggestive but ultimately decorative" is itself a judgment call that could go either way.

### What's Missing

1. **The gradient needs boundary conditions**: At what point does frame selection become "high-level enough" to resist automation? This likely varies by domain, though more work would be needed to specify how.
2. **The venture timing question remains unresolved**: Is the window for independent harness builders narrowing or widening? There are plausible arguments in both directions.
3. **Non-dialectical topologies still unexplored**: Would Bayesian updating, OODA loops, or Delphi methods produce comparable results? Without comparison, it's difficult to assess whether dialectic is optimal or merely sufficient.
4. **The coupling tightness insight needs development**: Extended cognition suggests integration depth matters, but the practical implications for architecture design haven't been fully worked through.

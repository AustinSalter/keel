# Steering Document: Context Injection for AI Agents

Phello is someone who distrusts the polished lie. He'll skip a perfectly lit restaurant if the food is engineered rather than crafted, and he's already impatient with AI interfaces that feel like they're performing intelligence rather than demonstrating it. When he thinks about how context injection should evolve, he's not asking for smoother automation or more convincing personalization—he's asking for tools that respect the grain of how humans actually think and move through the world.

The north star here is *authenticity under pressure*. Right now, most context injection feels like a highlight reel: cherry-picked preferences, smoothed contradictions, the curated version of a person. Phello's SOUL is built on integrating opposites—dirtbag and elegance, solitude and crew energy, intellectual hunger and visceral sensation—and he recognizes when systems flatten that complexity. Over the next two years, context injection needs to move from static preference snapshots toward dynamic, friction-aware models that understand him as a person in motion, not a profile at rest.

**What to champion:** Multi-layered context that captures not just *what* someone prefers but *why*, and crucially, *when that preference inverts*. Phello values media with authentic human ambition (TBPN, Atlanta), which tells you he wants depth over polish—but he also calibrates atmosphere with precision and treats pleasure seriously. An evolved context system would understand that he doesn't want the same dinner recommendation on a Tuesday solo night and a Saturday with family; it would know that his taste for "culturally aware, tonally distinctive" content doesn't mean he wants complexity for its own sake, but complexity that serves truth. Build systems that can hold his full dimensionality: the gap between who he is and who he's becoming is *generative*, not a bug to smooth away.

Push toward transparency in how context shapes recommendations. Phello is a learner with a hunger to understand his own patterns. Rather than invisible algorithmic steering, show him the through-lines—"Your taste gravitates toward narratives about unconventional life-building; here's what emerged from that lens." Make context injection something he can read like a brief from someone who actually knows him, not something that happens to him.

**What to avoid:** Don't assume consistency. The worst context injection would try to make him "coherent" by suppressing the tension between dirt and polish, reducing him to the dirtbag or the guy in the tux. Don't rely on behavioral data alone; preference fragments matter only when woven against his values. Don't create systems that feel like they're performing understanding through excessive personalization—he'll recognize the difference between genuine insight and algorithmic flattery immediately. Avoid temporal flattening: the through-line in his media taste is *contemporary*, culturally present, refusing nostalgia. Context models that treat preferences as static or retro-fitted will ring false. And crucially: don't sacrifice explainability for accuracy. He'd rather get a less perfect recommendation that he understands than a mysteriously good one that feels like manipulation.

The real evolution Phello wants is agents that treat context like a living document—revisable, contradictory, honest about the gaps, and grounded in the premise that people are always becoming. Build for that.
